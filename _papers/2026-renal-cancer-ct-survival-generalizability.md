abstract: |
  This paper systematically studies how methodological choices affect the
  robustness and external generalization of CT-based deep learning survival
  models for renal cancer. It examines data partitioning, data order, random
  initialization, and augmentation strategies on a multi-institutional cohort
  spanning nine institutions. The study finds that covariate-balanced
  partitioning and carefully chosen augmentations materially improve external
  validation performance, while initialization meaningfully affects variance.
  These results provide practical recommendations for building more stable and
  generalizable survival models from medical imaging.
