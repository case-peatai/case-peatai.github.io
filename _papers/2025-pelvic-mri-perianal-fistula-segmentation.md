abstract: |
  This paper introduces an automated pelvic MRI segmentation pipeline that
  combines nnU-Net with MedSAM for identifying perianal fistulae and the anal
  sphincter complex. The approach leverages self-configuring and foundation-
  model segmentation components to improve robustness on a difficult clinical
  anatomy problem. It is designed to support interventional guidance and
  surgical planning in Crohn's disease. The work demonstrates how task-specific
  and foundation-model methods can be integrated for clinically useful MRI
  analysis.
