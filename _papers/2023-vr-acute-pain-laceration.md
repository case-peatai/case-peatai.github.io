abstract: |
  This randomized controlled trial investigates whether virtual reality can
  reduce acute pain during laceration repair in emergency departments. Adult
  patients undergoing repair were studied in a real clinical setting to assess
  pain relief during the procedure. The work explores immersive VR as a
  practical non-pharmacologic intervention for emergency care. It adds clinical
  evidence on the use of interactive technology for procedural pain management.
