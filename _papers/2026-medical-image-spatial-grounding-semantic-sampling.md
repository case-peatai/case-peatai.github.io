abstract: |
  This work studies spatial grounding for vision-language models in 3D medical
  imaging, where anatomy, modality, slice direction, and coordinate systems
  create unique challenges. It introduces MIS-Ground, a benchmark for analyzing
  failure modes in medical image spatial grounding, and MIS-SemSam, an
  inference-time semantic sampling method that improves grounding accuracy
  without retraining. The paper evaluates how visual and textual prompting
  choices influence grounding performance across clinical imaging settings. It
  advances reproducible evaluation and practical improvement of medical VLM
  grounding.
